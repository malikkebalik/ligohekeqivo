Age, Biography and Wiki

Pedro Armendáriz was born on 9 May 1912 in Mexico City, Mexico. He was the son of a wealthy Mexican landowner and a French mother. He was educated in Mexico City and later attended the University of California, Los Angeles. Armendáriz began his acting career in the 1930s, appearing in Mexican films such as El Compadre Mendoza (1934) and La Mujer del Puerto (1935). He gained international recognition for his role in the 1940 film Allá en el Rancho Grande, which was the first Mexican film to be released in the United States. Armendáriz went on to appear in over 100 films, including The Magnificent Seven (1960), The Guns of Navarone (1961), and The Professionals (1966). He was known as the "Clark Gable of Mexico" for his rugged good looks and strong screen presence. Armendáriz was married twice and had four children. He died of cancer on 18 June, 1963 in Mexico City. As of 2021, Pedro Armendáriz's net worth is estimated to be around $2 million.
